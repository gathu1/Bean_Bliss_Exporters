SEEDLING

Coffee seedlings emerge from the seeds of ripe coffee cherries.
When these seeds are planted and nurtured, they grow into coffee plants.

GROWING

The seeds absorb moisture and begin to swell.
Germination occurs, and tiny coffee seedlings emerge.

HARVESTING

Skilled pickers selectively pluck only the ripest cherries by hand.
This labor-intensive process ensures optimal quality but requires more workers.

PROCESSING

The outer skin of the cherries is removed, revealing the inner layers.
Cherries are then fermented, and washed.

ROASTING

Coffee roasting is the magical transformation that turns green coffee beans into the aromatic, flavorful gems we grind and brew.

CUPPING

Coffee cupping is an artful ritual—a sensory exploration that reveals the soul of coffee.

BREWING

Whether drip, espresso, or French press, brewing extracts the essence.
The aroma fills the air, promising warmth, comfort, and a new day.
